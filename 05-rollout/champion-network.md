# Champion Network

## What is a Copilot Champion?

A volunteer developer on each team who:
- Becomes the go-to person for Copilot questions
- Shares tips and wins with their team
- Provides feedback to the enablement team
- Helps onboard new users

## Champion Selection Criteria

- [ ] Enthusiastic about AI/Copilot (early adopter mindset)
- [ ] Respected by peers (influence without authority)
- [ ] Willing to spend ~1 hr/week on champion activities
- [ ] Good communicator (can explain, not just use)
- [ ] Mix of seniority levels (senior + mid-level)

## Champion Responsibilities

| Activity | Frequency | Time |
|----------|:---------:|:----:|
| Attend champion sync meeting | Bi-weekly | 30 min |
| Share "tip of the week" in team channel | Weekly | 10 min |
| Help teammates with Copilot questions | Ad hoc | ~30 min/wk |
| Report adoption blockers to enablement team | As needed | 5 min |
| Test new features / provide early feedback | As released | 15 min |

## Champion Enablement

| Resource | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Private champion Teams/Slack channel | Direct line to enablement team |
| Early access to new Copilot features | GitHub beta programs |
| Monthly "What's new in Copilot" briefing | 15 min update from enablement team |
| Champion badge / recognition | Swag, shout-out in all-hands |

## Champion Program Cadence

| Week | Activity |
|:----:|----------|
| 1 | Champion kickoff — training + expectations |
| 2 | Champions onboard their teams |
| 3 | First champion sync — share wins + blockers |
| 4 | Tip-sharing campaign launches |
| 6 | Champion retrospective — what's working? |
| 8 | Expand champion network to Wave 2 teams |
| 12 | Quarterly review — metrics + recognition |

## Scaling the Network

Target ratio: **1 champion per 10–15 developers**

| Org Size | Champions Needed |
|:--------:|:----------------:|
| 50 devs | 4–5 |
| 100 devs | 7–10 |
| 500 devs | 35–50 |
| 1000+ devs | 70+ (organize by department) |
